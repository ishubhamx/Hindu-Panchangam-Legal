Sacred Knowledge

Ancient wisdom for the modern world

The Hindu Panchangam is a sacred almanac rooted in Vedic astronomy and astrology, offering precise calculations of celestial movements to determine auspicious timings, festivals, and cosmic events.

Spanning thousands of years of astronomical observation, it serves as the spiritual compass for rituals, marriages, journeys, and daily life — aligning human activity with the rhythms of the cosmos.

Pancha + Anga

The Five Limbs of Time

Panchangam derives from two Sanskrit words — Pancha (five) and Anga (limbs). Together they form the five sacred elements of Vedic timekeeping.

🌙 Tithi Lunar Day The lunar day, defined by the angular distance between Sun and Moon. There are 30 tithis in a lunar month.
⭐ Vara Day of Week The day of the week, each presided over by a celestial deity and planetary lord.
✨ Nakshatra Lunar Mansion The 27 lunar mansions marking the Moon's position along the ecliptic on a given day.
☀️ Yoga Auspicious Period The sum of the longitudes of the Sun and Moon divided into 27 yogas, each with specific qualities.
🔮 Karana Half Tithi Half a tithi, the sub-division of lunar days used for determining suitable activities.
